Research

Research themes centered on robust neural decoding and interpretable modeling.

My work connects machine learning, neural signal processing, and translational BCI systems, with projects spanning intracortical recordings, ECoG, and EEG.

01

Cross-day stable intracortical decoding

Current BrainGate work studies representational drift and decoder degradation across sessions in human intracortical BCI.

  • Prototype-aligned contrastive decoding for 13-class cross-session evaluation
  • Cross-session macro-F1 improved from 0.76 to 0.86, above LDA-HMM (~0.80)
  • LFP-spike analysis, optimal transport, and flow matching for reduced recalibration

02

Dynamics-aware motion decoding

NeuroScribe 2.0 reframes continuous neural decoding as stable control inference under explicit dynamical priors.

  • Conv-Transformer plus state-space DMP decoder for smooth trajectory rollout
  • Behavior-derived subject prior for personalized and robust decoding
  • Validated on ECoG and multi-session spiking benchmarks

03

Cross-modal and interpretable neural representation learning

Earlier projects developed joint latent models and interpretable feature encoders for motor imagery, movement decoding, and epilepsy analysis.

  • Cross-modal ECoG and movement alignment with contrastive learning
  • PCA, RDM, and RSA for representation analysis in EEG decoding
  • State-space modeling and HMM-based neural decoding pipelines

Research Appointment

Graduate Student Researcher, BrainGate

10/2025 - Present

Advisor: Prof. John Simeral · Providence, US

Cross-Day Stable Multi-Gesture Decoding for Intracortical BCI

  • Investigating longitudinal neural representational drift and cross-session decoder degradation in human intracortical BCI datasets.
  • Developed a prototype-aligned contrastive decoder that improved held-out cross-session 13-class macro-F1 from 0.76 to 0.86, outperforming LDA-HMM (~0.80 macro-F1).
  • Extending decoder stabilization with LFP-spike feature analysis, optimal transport, and flow matching to reduce recalibration and support scalable multi-gesture BCI.

Neural Representational Drift Modeling in Human Intracortical BCI

  • Developing flow matching and optimal transport-based models to capture neural distribution shifts across sessions and align new-session activity to a reference-day distribution.
  • Evaluating whether drift-aware inverse mapping improves cross-day decoding performance and reduces recalibration.
  • Studying latent-space drift geometry and whether learned dynamics follow optimal-transport-like paths for long-term robust decoder design.

Research Appointment

Research Assistant, Tsinghua Laboratory of Neural Engineering

10/2024 - 06/2025

Advisor: Prof. Bo Hong · Beijing, China

NeuroScribe 2.0: A Bio-Inspired Neural Decoding Model

  • Reframed continuous neural decoding as stable control inference under an explicit dynamical prior, replacing unconstrained sequence regression with structured trajectory generation.
  • Developed a Conv-Transformer plus state-space DMP decoder that infers low-dimensional movement controls from neural history and rolls them out into smooth, dynamically consistent kinematic trajectories.
  • Evaluated on ECoG and multi-session spiking benchmarks, including M2 (R^2 = 0.44, RMSE = 0.70, MSJ = 1.62) and MouseTrack (R^2 = 0.42, RMSE = 0.72), with slower degradation than Transformer, LSTM, RNN, Kalman, NoMAD.

Cross-Modal Representation Learning for ECoG and Movement Signals

  • Developed a shared latent-space framework jointly modeling ECoG and wrist movement signals.
  • Applied contrastive learning to strengthen correspondence between neural and motion representations.
  • Built an end-to-end architecture supporting both decoding and cross-modal signal generation for real-time BCI settings.

Probabilistic State-Space Neural Decoder

  • Designed a two-layer decoder integrating HMM with state-specific LDS to model continuous hand gestures from epidural ECoG.
  • Implemented probabilistic state fusion to dynamically weight LDS predictions across six gestures.
  • Achieved 0.068 MSE versus a 0.166 RNN baseline, improving real-time decoder stability by about 59%.

Motor Imagery Decoding and Representation Analysis in EEG

Trained TFSICNet to decode motor imagery classes from EEG, then used PCA, representation visualization, RDMs, and RSA to analyze class separability.

NeuroScribe: Physics-Inspired EEG-to-Trajectory Generation for BCI

01/2024 - 08/2024 · Advisor: Dr. Zhongyuan Lai · Fudan University

Developed an EEG-to-trajectory framework combining STFEnc with an Auto-DMP decoder, achieving the lowest MSE on SMR-BCI and SignEEG v1.0 with the smoothest trajectories among compared baselines.

STFEnc: A Novel Deep Encoder for Brain-Computer Interface Based on Interpretable Brain Features

01/2024 - 06/2024 · Advisor: Dr. Zhongyuan Lai · Fudan University

Preprocessed multiple EEG datasets with Python-MNE, ICA, and filtering, and developed a PyTorch encoder integrating GCNN and connectome information, reaching 80.78% average accuracy versus 74.24% for EEGNet.

Epilepsy Classification and Prediction Using Deep Learning and State-Space Modeling

10/2023 - 04/2024 · Advisor: Prof. Yao Lu · Sun Yat-Sen University

Built an epilepsy analysis pipeline with TSICNet and HMM classification on CHB-MIT and HUH neonatal EEG datasets, reaching 99.72% average accuracy versus 98.75% for EEGNet.
