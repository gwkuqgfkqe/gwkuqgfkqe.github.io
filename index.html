Research

Research areas that define my current work.

My projects connect machine learning, neural signal processing, and translational neurotechnology across intracortical recordings, ECoG, and EEG.

01

Neural Decoding

Designing models that decode movement and behavior from neural activity with stronger robustness, smoother dynamics, and better cross-session performance.

02

Representational Drift

Studying how neural representations change over time and how those shifts degrade decoding performance in longitudinal BCI settings.

03

Longitudinal Intracortical BCI

Building cross-day stable decoding pipelines that reduce recalibration and preserve performance in human intracortical brain-computer interfaces.

04

Computational Neuroscience

Using representation learning, state-space models, and drift-aware alignment methods to better explain neural dynamics and support scalable BCI design.

Research Plan

A clear agenda for future PhD work.

Based on my current Brown, THNE, and Fudan projects, my research plan is organized around three connected directions.

Direction 1

Cross-day stable neural decoding

Develop longitudinal intracortical decoders that preserve multi-gesture performance across sessions with less recalibration and stronger robustness.

Direction 2

Drift-aware alignment and modeling

Use representational drift analysis, optimal transport, and flow-matching methods to map new-session activity back to a stable reference space.

Direction 3

Interpretable dynamics across neural modalities

Extend dynamics-based neural inference from intracortical BCI to ECoG and EEG, linking robust decoding with interpretable neural structure and control-oriented models.

Research Appointment

Graduate Student Researcher, BrainGate

Oct 2025 - Present

Advisor: Prof. John Simeral, Brown University · Providence, RI

Cross-Day Stable Neural Decoding and Neural Drift Modeling in Human Intracortical BCI

  • Investigating longitudinal neural representational drift and cross-session decoder degradation in human intracortical BCI datasets.
  • Developed a prototype-aligned contrastive decoder that improved held-out cross-session 13-class macro-F1 from 0.76 to 0.86, surpassing the lab's strongest classical baseline, LDA-HMM (~0.80), on the same benchmark.
  • Extending decoder stabilization with LFP-spike feature analysis, optimal transport, and flow-matching methods to reduce recalibration and improve robustness in scalable multi-gesture BCI.
  • Developing drift-aware alignment models to capture session-to-session neural distribution shifts, map new-session activity to a reference-day distribution, and examine whether latent drift follows plausible transport-like trajectories.

Research Appointment

Research Assistant, Tsinghua Laboratory of Neural Engineering (THNE)

Oct 2024 - Jun 2025

Advisor: Prof. Bo Hong, Tsinghua University · Beijing, China

NeuroScribe 2.0: A Bio-Inspired Neural Decoding Model

  • Reframed continuous neural decoding as stable control inference under an explicit dynamical prior, replacing unconstrained sequence regression with structured trajectory generation.
  • Developed a Conv-Transformer plus state-space DMP decoder that inferred low-dimensional movement controls from neural history and rolled them out into smooth, dynamically consistent kinematic trajectories.
  • Introduced a behavior-derived subject prior learned from training kinematics to personalize decoding and improve robustness without requiring held-out neural or behavioral labels.
  • Achieved best or tied-best decoding accuracy with substantially smoother trajectories, including M2 (R^2 = 0.44, RMSE = 0.70, MSJ = 1.62) and MouseTrack (R^2 = 0.42, RMSE = 0.72).

Cross-Modal Representation Learning for ECoG and Movement Signals

  • Developed a cross-modal framework that jointly modeled ECoG and wrist-movement signals in a shared latent space.
  • Applied contrastive learning to improve alignment between ECoG and motion representations, strengthening cross-modal correspondence and enhancing motor-intention decoding.
  • Built an end-to-end architecture that supported both decoding and cross-modal signal generation for real-time BCI applications.

Probabilistic State-Space Neural Decoder

  • Designed a two-layer neural decoder that integrated HMM with state-specific LDS to model continuous hand gestures from epidural ECoG signals.
  • Implemented HMM-based motor-phase decoding and used mixture-of-experts LDS modules to generate finger-trajectory predictions across six gestures.
  • Developed a probabilistic state-fusion mechanism that dynamically weighted LDS predictions using HMM output probabilities.
  • Achieved an MSE of 0.068 versus 0.166 for an RNN baseline (~59% reduction), improving decoder stability in real-time settings.

Research Appointment

Research Assistant, Fudan University

Jan 2024 - Jun 2025

Advisor: Dr. Zhongyuan Lai, Fudan University · Remote

NeuroScribe: Physics-Inspired EEG-to-Trajectory Generation for BCI

  • Developed an end-to-end EEG-to-trajectory framework that combined the STFEnc encoder with an Auto-DMP decoder for smooth, dynamically constrained motion generation.
  • Predicted compact Auto-DMP parameters from EEG signals instead of directly regressing trajectory samples, improving robustness and parameter efficiency.
  • Achieved the lowest MSE on SMR-BCI (3.42) and SignEEG v1.0 (9.24), while also producing the smoothest trajectories relative to Transformer, LSTM, RNN, EEGNet, Kalman filter, and direct-regression baselines.

STFEnc: A Novel Deep Encoder for Brain-Computer Interface Based on Interpretable Brain Features

  • Preprocessed EEG data from BNCI 2014-001 Motor Imagery, High-Gamma, and Object datasets using Python-MNE, ICA, and filtering.
  • Developed the STFEnc neural encoder to capture spatial EEG features, integrating GCNNs and connectome-informed priors in PyTorch.
  • Extracted time- and frequency-domain EEG features using convolutional kernels and attention mechanisms.
  • Achieved 80.78% average accuracy versus 74.24% for EEGNet, improving decoding stability and reliability.

Epilepsy Classification and Prediction Using Deep Learning and State-Space Modeling
